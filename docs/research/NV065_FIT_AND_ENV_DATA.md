## Part 1 — DON26BZ03-NV065 (Adaptive Sensor Management) fit assessment

### 1.1 The requirement (NV065 intent)
Dynamic **tasking / resource allocation across multi-sensor naval systems under adversarial conditions** — the "which sensor looks where, when, and at what revisit rate" decision layer. NAVSEA · DON SBIR 26.3 / R3 · opens 6/24, closes 7/22 (same BZ03 window as NV061/063). It is the **action side** of the object-level situational-awareness story NV063 (is the track anomalous) + NV061 (where is the track going) already tell.

### 1.2 What's reusable from the NV063/NV061 stack (the leverage)
NV065 is not a from-scratch bid for us — it sits *on top of* the SA picture NV063/NV061 produce. Concretely reusable:

| Reusable asset | Status | How NV065 uses it |
|---|---|---|
| The **fused track picture** (AIS+ADS-B+radar → one contact list) | [PARTIAL] (AIS [BUILT]; ADS-B capture [BUILT]; radar/fusion GT [PROPOSED]) | The tasking layer reasons over *the same* fused contacts — sensor-tasking value is "look harder at the track the anomaly/trajectory layer is least certain about." |
| **Cold-start Pattern-of-Life + explainable alerts** (`demo/ais_pol.py`) | [BUILT] | Anomaly score + confidence is the **priority signal**: a low-confidence anomaly (e.g. an AIS dark-gap with no corroborating sensor) is exactly the contact a sensor-tasking *recommendation* should surface ("cue radar/EO on MMSI X — uncorroborated dark-gap"). The `demo/ais_pol.py::detect` dark-gap detector already emits "cue another sensor" as its recommended action — that is the NV065 seam, today, in code. |
| **NV061 trajectory + (proposed) calibrated uncertainty** (`models/nv061/`) | [BUILT] baseline; conformal intervals [PROPOSED] | Predicted-position **uncertainty region** is the cleanest tasking-value function: task the sensor where the track's future-position covariance is largest / where two hypotheses branch at a waypoint. The marginal cost to *frame* NV065 on this stack is low — it reuses the contact picture, the priority signal, the uncertainty, the record, and the deploy. That is the honest case for bidding it.

### 1.3 The HONEST gap (do not paper over)
**THESEUS does not do active sensor tasking today. It is a detection / forecast / record layer.** Specifically:
- There is **no [BUILT] sensor-resource-allocation engine** — no scheduler, no POMDP/info-gain tasking policy, no closed loop to a radar/EO controller. The dark-gap detector's "cue another sensor" line is an *explainable recommendation string*, not a tasking algorithm.
- THESEUS has **no sensor-control interface** and, by the SWAN-side rail, will not have one that touches the combat system in Phase I.
- NV065's "adversarial conditions" (jamming, spoofing, deceptive maneuver forcing sensor-resource contention) is **[PROPOSED]** research for us; the spoof/position-jump detector is the closest [BUILT] adjacency, not an adversarial-tasking solution.
- We have **no naval multi-sensor tasking benchmark** and no open one exists (same gap as the naval-TP benchmark in `docs/research/datasets/E_TRAJECTORY_PREDICTION_REPORT.md`); Phase-I eval would be simulator-driven (Stone Soup multi-sensor + synthetic tasking scenarios).

### 1.4 Rails (carry into any NV065 pitch — non-negotiable)
- **Advisory sensor-tasking RECOMMENDATIONS, human-in-command.** THESEUS *recommends* "cue sensor Y on track X because its predicted-position uncertainty / anomaly-corroboration gap is highest"; the watch team tasks the sensor. THESEUS **never** autonomously controls a sensor. Banned framing: "AI tasks the radar" / "autonomous sensor management."
- **SWAN-side; compose-not-replace.** Recommendations ride alongside the combat system via certified data pipes (the Maven/Palantir pattern), never embedded in the sensor-control loop in Phase I. Live closed-loop tasking = Phase III, prime-mediated, NAVSEA-certified.
- **Every recommendation + every operator decision sealed** into the tamper-evident record (accreditation by construction).

### 1.5 Verdict — MEDIUM-to-STRONG (lower than NV063/NV061; bid as the stretch/Phase-II story)
- **Fit verdict: MEDIUM-STRONG.** Strong *conceptual* fit and strong *leverage* on the NV063/NV061 stack (shared picture + uncertainty + record + deploy), but a genuine *capability* gap (no tasking engine today). It is honestly weaker than NV063 (STRONG, bullseye) and NV061 (STRONG-adjacent) — consistent with the existing `NAVSEA_SBIR_TOPICS.md` "MEDIUM-STRONG" read.
- **Recommended posture:** treat NV065 as the **Phase-II autonomy extension** of NV063/NV061, *or* a thin standalone Phase-I scoped to a recommendation engine (below). Do **not** over-promise a tasking-control system in Phase I.
- **Phase-I scope (if bid standalone):** an **advisory sensor-tasking recommendation cell** — a value-of-information ranker over the fused track list that turns NV061 predicted-position uncertainty + NV063 anomaly-corroboration gaps into ranked "cue sensor Y on track X" recommendations with explainable reasons, sealed into the record. Eval on a Stone Soup multi-sensor simulation (info-gain vs a round-robin / coverage-only baseline). No live sensor control.
- **Phase-II extension:** adversarial-condition tasking (jam/spoof-aware re-allocation), closed-loop recommendation→accept→re-rank against a simulated sensor suite, calibrated tasking under deception.
- **Sequencing call:** lead with **NV063 + NV061** (shared stack, strongest fit, real receipts). Carry **NV065 as the explicit Phase-II vision** inside the NV061/NV063 volumes (the uncertainty→tasking thread), and bid it standalone only if team bandwidth allows a credible recommendation-engine Phase-I. The bid is strongest framed as "the SA picture earns the right to recommend where to look next" — not as a new autonomy product.

## Part 2 — Open environmental-context data (currents / SST / waves)

**Why this matters for THESEUS:** the NV063 Pattern-of-Life cell and the NV061 trajectory model are **kinematics-only today** (`[lat, lon, sog, cog, ts, mmsi]`). Environmental context is the highest-value feature extension: surface currents disambiguate *drift vs. powered loiter*, sea-state explains the *speed envelope*, and SST fronts explain *benign fishing loiter*. The canonical env-aware open dataset (Brest/Ray-2019) is **CC BY-NC-SA → research-only** (`docs/research/datasets/DATASETS.md` §6) — so the sources below are the **commercial-clean** path to the same env features for a shipped product + a for-profit SBIR.

### 2.2 How env context concretely upgrades the [BUILT] models
- **Currents → loiter/drift discrimination [PROPOSED feature on a BUILT detector].** `demo/ais_pol.py::detect` flags loiter when a vessel transited (>3 kn) then sat <0.5 kn. A vessel adrift in a 2–3 kn current has near-zero propulsion but a non-zero, current-aligned SOG vector. Differencing the contact's velocity against the local HYCOM/CMEMS surface current separates **powered station-keeping (suspicious — surveillance/rendezvous)** from **drifting (benign / disabled / fishing set)** — fewer nuisance alerts, better explanations.
- **Currents → NV061 set/drift correction [PROPOSED].** Predicting in a water-relative frame (subtract current, extrapolate STW/heading, re-add current) is the physical reason Brest/Ray-2019 bundles env layers; it's the cleanest long-horizon accuracy lever beyond raw kinematics, reported as gain vs. the constant-velocity floor.
- **Sea-state (WW3 / CMEMS WAV / NDBC) → speed-envelope conditioning [PROPOSED].** The NV063 cell learns a per-class **99th-percentile SOG envelope** in-situ. Vessels voluntarily slow in heavy seas (speed loss in waves), so the "normal" max shrinks with Hs. Conditioning the envelope on significant wave height stops weather from masking real over/under-speed anomalies (and explains them).
- **SST fronts → benign-fishing context [PROPOSED].** Thermal fronts concentrate fishing; SST-front proximity is a feature that reclassifies front-adjacent loiter/zig-zag as consistent-with-fishing rather than anomalous.

### 2.3 DDIL note (HARD constraint — carry into the bid)
**Every gridded cloud feed above is a shore / build-time SEED only.** HYCOM, CMEMS, and NOAA ERDDAP/NOMADS are internet services; **none can reach an airgapped ship at sea.** They pre-stage the env-conditioned model **ashore** — the model learns the env-dependence (current-corrected drift logic, sea-state-conditioned envelope, SST-front feature) and carries that *learned dependence* into the UDS/Zarf airgap bundle. At sea the only live env inputs are **own-ship sensors** (anemometer, the ship's own met/oceanographic package, radar-derived sea-state) plus the **last forecast grids cached at sail**. **NDBC buoy data is likewise shore-telemetered** — build-time ground-truth/calibration, not an at-sea runtime feed. This is exactly the `DATASETS.md` §5.4 rule ("train ashore, sustain underway"); the env feeds extend it, they don't break it.

### 2.4 Honest gaps
- **No env feed reaches the airgap at runtime** (§2.3) — env-awareness is a *trained-in* property + cached forecast, not a live service; state this plainly or the Red Team's "name a real at-sea feed" gate bites.
- **Model currents are forecasts, not truth** — HYCOM/CMEMS surface currents carry error (eddies, coastal/tidal regimes); NDBC + own-ship sensors are the in-situ correction, and currents help *most* offshore/open-ocean, *less* in tidal/littoral water.
- **Env-aware gain is [PROPOSED], not measured** — we have not yet quantified the loiter-precision or NV061-nmi improvement from adding currents/sea-state; that is a Phase-I experiment (ablation: kinematics-only vs. +currents vs. +sea-state vs. +SST), reported vs. the CV/Kalman + no-env baselines, HONESTY-FIRST.
- **Attribution string on CMEMS** — the only license obligation among the four; HYCOM/NOAA/NDBC are public-domain clean. Keep the CC BY-NC-SA Brest/Ray-2019 env set in the R&D enclave (never shipped) per `DATASETS.md`.
