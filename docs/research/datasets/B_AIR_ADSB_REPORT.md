## Top datasets — access mechanics (3–6 sentences each)

**OpenSky Trino Historical DB.** The deepest open ADS-B archive (full state vectors, global, 2016→present). Access is via a Trino/Minio SQL endpoint after you verify email and submit "Request Data Access." Crucially, the license grants use only for non-profit research/education, **commercial internal testing & evaluation**, or **government purposes** — and *"private or commercial entities must contact us for a license"* for anything beyond eval. The §6 IP clause makes inventions developed *for commercial use* using the data **joint property of OpenSky** unless you get written exemption. For THESEUS this is excellent for cold-start "normal"-pattern learning and offline eval (especially under the SBIR "government purposes" prong), but the data must **not** be embedded in or shipped with the product, and a legal pass on the IP clause is required.

**ADS-B Exchange historical archive.** Unique because it is **unfiltered and includes military/blocked aircraft** that FlightAware/Flightradar24 suppress — directly on-point for NV063 "anomalous military/air activity around a Navy ship." Free access is limited to the **first-of-the-month** datasets for testing/evaluation; production use requires a **paid commercial agreement** (pull from their S3 buckets or push to your cloud). Formats: `readsb-hist` 5-second global snapshots, per-aircraft `traces` (and `hires-traces` at 2/sec), `flights`, `ACAS` (TCAS alerts), and `operations`. The free samples are enough to prototype the ADS-B fusion lane; budget for a license at productization.

**airplanes.live REST API.** The strongest **free, unfiltered** live feed (incl. military + MLAT), a community successor to the original ADS-BX ethos. Documented REST API (`/api-guide/`, field descriptions, docs). The free tier is intended for non-commercial/hobby use; **commercial use routes through `contact@airplanes.live`** (a RapidAPI plan is advertised as "coming soon"). Good for live coastal/at-sea capture during development and demos; not a bulk historical source, and a commercial agreement is needed before it touches a shipped product.

**TartanAviation (CMU).** The best **license-clean, multi-modal** asset: **image + ATC speech + ADS-B trajectory** concurrently collected at two Pittsburgh airports, **CC BY 4.0** (commercial OK with attribution). For THESEUS the value is the **fusion template**, not maritime content: it demonstrates time-synchronizing tracks to sensor frames and projecting 3D ADS-B positions into an image plane (PnP/extrinsics) — the same co-registration THESEUS must do across AIS/ADS-B/radar. Download via Python scripts on GitHub `castacks/TartanAviation` (mirrored on Zenodo, DOI 10.5281/zenodo.14699102), with sample/partial-download support. Air-only and US-GA-terminal; confirm the Zenodo record's license field matches the article's CC-BY before shipping derived artifacts.

**Mendeley GPS-Spoofing UAS dataset.** **CC BY 4.0** (commercial OK), 247 MB, with **labeled authentic vs. three spoofing-attack classes** — useful seed for the "anomalous behavior / spoofed track" side of THESEUS's anomaly model. Caveat: it is **GPS-receiver signal-level** data (8 channels × 13 features) emulating a UAS, **not ADS-B network tracks**, so it informs the spoofing-classifier concept rather than dropping into the ADS-B ingest path.

## Access / gotchas — LICENSE-FIRST notes

- **OpenSky is the big landmine.** Its "Open" Zenodo badge is misleading: the actual Data License Agreement permits only non-profit research/education, **commercial internal testing & evaluation**, or **government purposes** — **not** shipping data inside a commercial product and **not** redistribution. The **§6 IP clause** can make commercially-developed inventions *joint property of OpenSky* absent a written exemption. For an SBIR (government-funded) R&D phase the "government purposes" prong is plausibly usable, **but treat product embedding as prohibited and get a legal read + (for production) a commercial license**. Cite OpenSky per their required attribution if you publish.
- **AIS↔ADS-B joint fusion datasets are essentially nonexistent in the open.** No verified open dataset ships maritime AIS and air ADS-B co-registered on one frame. THESEUS must **fuse them itself** by time/space-aligning a maritime AIS source (Category A) with an ADS-B source (this category). TartanAviation is the closest *methodological* template (multi-sensor↔track co-registration) but is air-only. **Treat fusion ground-truth as a build item, not a download.**
- **"Unfiltered/military" is the differentiator.** Only **ADS-B Exchange** and **airplanes.live** retain military/blocked aircraft; mainstream feeds (FlightAware/FR24) suppress them. NV063's threat picture needs the unfiltered feed → plan for one of these (both have commercial tiers).
- **Free ≠ commercial.** Cleanly commercial-OK today: **TartanAviation** and **Mendeley GPS-Spoofing** (both CC-BY 4.0). Commercial-OK **only via paid license**: ADS-B Exchange, airplanes.live. **Not** for product: OpenSky (research/gov/eval only).
- **Tooling is safe.** The `traffic` library / pyOpenSky / pyModeS are permissively licensed (use freely in the ingest pipeline); only the *data* they fetch carries the upstream license.
- **Wingbits sample** license detail is **UNVERIFIED** beyond "free sample" — confirm before any non-eval use.
