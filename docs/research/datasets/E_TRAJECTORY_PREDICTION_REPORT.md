## Top datasets — access mechanics & what baseline each enables

**1. TrAISformer `ct_dma` (CIA-Oceanix).** `git clone` the repo; the processed dataset ships under `./data/ct_dma/` as a pickle with rows `[lat, lon, sog, cog, unix_timestamp, mmsi]` (DMA-sourced; preprocessing code is the GeoTrackNet `csv2pkl.py`). Run `trAISformer.py` to train+eval out-of-the-box (errors printed in km; paper reports nautical miles). This is the single fastest path to a **published SOTA baseline you must beat for NV061** — a generative transformer that emits a *distribution* over future positions (not a point), which is exactly the calibrated-forecast posture NV061 wants. License is **CeCILL-C**: commercial use is permitted, but if you modify *their* source you must make those modifications' source available (copyleft on the software, not on a model you train independently).

**2. Brest / Ray et al. 2019 (Zenodo 1167595).** Download per-file or `files-archive` (1.8 GB total; `[P1] AIS Data.zip` ≈ 201 MB is the AIS core). Designed for PostgreSQL+PostGIS with bundled integration queries. Its differentiator for NV061 is the **aligned environmental + geographic layers** (ocean/weather, coastline, ports, EEZ), enabling *environment-aware* long-horizon forecasting and a realistic Brittany ROI. **License is the one open question** — the Zenodo record reads "Open Access" but exposes no explicit CC tag in metadata; the companion Data-in-Brief article is CC BY. Treat as research-safe; **license-clear before any commercial/delivered use.**

**3. Piraeus AIS (Zenodo 5562629).** Free download, but **CC BY-NC-SA 4.0** — the NonCommercial + ShareAlike clauses make it **research/eval-only**; it must be firewalled from the THESEUS product and from any SBIR data deliverable. Value: 244M records over 2.5 years in a dense port (congestion realism for the NV063-adjacent "congested maritime" condition) plus pre-computed trajectory *synopses* (critical-point summaries) you can reuse for compression on the edge.

**4. RNN TP evaluation suite (Slaughter et al. 2024, isaacOnline/ships).** Clone the repo for a reproducible **baseline harness**: it benchmarks RNN/seq2seq variants across MarineCadastre (US) and Norwegian AIS with **ADE/FDE** at multiple horizons and ablates which features/architectures actually move the needle. Use it to (a) stand up the NV061 baseline grid quickly and (b) justify feature choices in the proposal with cited evidence rather than assertion.

**5. Conformal-interval layer (method, not dataset).** NV061 explicitly wants **calibrated intervals**. There is no off-the-shelf maritime conformal benchmark, so the play is: take the TrAISformer/`ct_dma` or Brest split, hold out a calibration set, and wrap **split-conformal or adaptive-conformal** (per arXiv 2508.13362) around the forecaster to get distribution-free per-horizon coverage. The Bornholm collision-probability paper (MDPI Sensors) is a worked maritime example of turning AIS forecasts into calibrated risk.

## TOP PICKS for THESEUS

1. **TrAISformer `ct_dma`** — the load-bearing open AIS-TP **benchmark + SOTA baseline**; permissive-enough (CeCILL-C) for product use; ships a probabilistic forecaster aligned to NV061's "intervals not points." **Start here.**
2. **Brest / Ray et al. 2019** — best **environment-aware** long-horizon ROI; pair its env layers with `ct_dma` kinematics. Clear the license first.
3. **isaacOnline/ships (2024)** — the **baseline + ablation harness** (ADE/FDE, feature/architecture grid) to make NV061 results rigorous and citable.
4. **Constant-Velocity / Nearly-Constant-Velocity Kalman** *(not a dataset — the mandatory floor)* — NV061's real bar is **"beat the kinematic baseline."** Implement CV/CTRV/Kalman as the null model on every split; an ML forecaster that doesn't beat NCV at the target horizon doesn't earn its place in track management. State ADE/FDE gain vs. NCV in the proposal.

**Why this set:** it gives (a) a permissive benchmark to ship against, (b) the environmental context for long-horizon realism, (c) a citable baseline methodology, and (d) the conformal wrapper that satisfies the "calibrated" half of NV061 — without depending on any NonCommercial data in the delivered system.

## Access / gotchas

- **License-first ranking for *delivered/commercial* use:** TrAISformer code (CeCILL-C, commercial-OK w/ copyleft on mods) and US/Norway gov AIS (open) are clean. **Brest = VERIFY license. Piraeus = NC, research-only. Off-domain road sets = NC, research-only + domain-gap.** Keep NC data strictly in the R&D enclave.
- **CeCILL-C nuance:** it's a French free-software license (GPL/LGPL-compatible family). Commercial use and redistribution are allowed; the obligation is to publish source of modifications *to TrAISformer's own code*. A model you train on the `ct_dma` data using your own code is **not** a derivative of their software.
- **Metrics to standardize (so NV061 numbers are comparable):** report **ADE** and **FDE** (haversine, meters→km→nm), **minADE/minFDE_k** for multimodal forecasts, **NLL/PICP+MPIW** for calibrated intervals, all per-horizon (e.g., 5/10/30/60 min and multi-hour). Always include the **NCV/Kalman baseline** column.
- **Horizon mismatch trap:** maritime TP is multi-minute-to-multi-hour; road-forecast leaderboards are sub-10-second. Borrow road benchmarks' *metric conventions* and conformal recipes, never their data or horizons.
- **Overlap with Category A (do not re-derive):** MarineCadastre (US, public domain), Danish DMA, Norwegian Kystverket and OMTAD are A's raw sources. Category E consumes them only through the *prediction harnesses* above (splits/features/metrics/baselines). Don't duplicate A's raw-source catalog.
- **No open *naval* TP benchmark exists** — every open AIS-TP set is commercial/fishing traffic. Navy-relevant maneuvering (evasion, formation, sensor-cued intercept) needs expert annotation or simulation (see Category F sim/synth). Flag this honestly in the proposal.
