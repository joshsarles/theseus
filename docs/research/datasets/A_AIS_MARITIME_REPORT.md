# THESEUS Dataset Research — Category A: AIS Vessel-Trajectory + Maritime Pattern-of-Life + Anomaly-Detection Benchmarks

**Scope:** Open-source / open-access AIS vessel-trajectory data, maritime Pattern-of-Life (POL) corpora, and labeled maritime-anomaly benchmarks usable to build, train, and benchmark THESEUS (airgapped onboard maritime decision-support substrate; AIS+ADS-B+radar fusion; cold-start POL with no large historical DB; explainable anomaly flagging; tamper-evident sealed records; DDIL). Target SBIR topic **NV063** (anomalous-behavior detection in congested maritime environments using AIS+ADS-B+radar fusion, explainable AI, no historical DB).

**HONESTY-FIRST notes:** Every row below was URL-verified on 2026-06-17 unless explicitly marked UNVERIFIED. License/commercial-use status is stated precisely; anything non-commercial or unclear is flagged LOUDLY. "Commercial-OK" means the license permits use in a commercial product and/or a (for-profit) SBIR deliverable.

## Per-dataset access mechanics (top datasets)

**MarineCadastre.gov AIS (US).** Bulk historical data is downloaded as yearly, UTM-zone-partitioned ZIP→CSV from `https://marinecadastre.gov/ais/` (no login). The interactive `accessais/` map lets you draw an Area-of-Interest and email-order a clipped extract (2 GB cap), but that order service is intermittently down — default to the bulk yearly zips. As a US Government work the data carry no copyright (public domain), so they are safe for a commercial product and a for-profit SBIR deliverable. Fields are pre-extracted and standardized (position, time, ship type, speed, length, beam, draft, transceiver class), which minimizes parsing work for the fusion pipeline.

**Danish Maritime Authority (DMA) AIS.** Historical data is free and openly downloadable as zipped CSV from `http://aisdata.ais.dk/` (the path the dma.dk page links to; formerly `web.ais.dk/aisdata/`). Files are organized by date; you pull the zips directly over HTTP and decompress. Coverage runs 2006→present and is continuously updated. The dma.dk page states the data are "free for down-load"; before shipping, confirm exact reuse terms on the DMA "AIS data management policy" page (linked from the AIS-data page) — it is widely used commercially but the explicit license string should be captured for the SBIR compliance file.

**Ushant / Brest TSS AIS (figshare).** One-click 125 MB download from figshare (DOI `10.6084/m9.figshare.8966273`) or `git clone` the `rtavenar/ushant_ais` GitHub mirror. **Licensed CC BY 4.0 (verified)** — commercial use permitted with attribution. 18,603 trajectories / 7M+ points over a structured two-lane traffic-separation scheme make it an ideal clean "normal" baseline for cold-start POL and for synthesizing lane/direction-violation anomalies.

**Piraeus AIS (Zenodo 6323416).** Download the component ZIPs individually (or "Download all") from `https://zenodo.org/records/6323416` — total ≈4.8 GB across `unipi_ais_dynamic_2017/2018/2019`, `ais_static`, `geodata` (shapefiles), `noaa_weather`, and `unipi_ais_dynamic_synopses`. Published with the Elsevier *Data in Brief* paper (DOI `10.1016/j.dib.2021.107782`), which by Data-in-Brief policy is CC BY 4.0; confirm the explicit Zenodo license tag for the compliance file. The bundled weather + pre-computed synopses make it the strongest single source for multi-modal fusion experiments in a congested port.

**Norwegian Coastal Administration (Kystverket).** The open component is free, requires no registration, and is governed by **NLOD 2.0 (commercial-OK)**. Access the live stream at IP `153.44.253.27:5631`, via BarentsWatch open-data APIs, or the `https://ais-public.kystverket.no/` download beta. Note the open feed excludes fishing vessels <15 m and recreational craft <45 m; full coverage requires a registration form with a stated purpose and approval (and carries third-party-distribution restrictions on the excluded vessel classes).

**OMTAD (anomaly benchmark).** The MODSIM-2021 paper PDF is openly readable at `mssanz.org.au`; the 2025 NeurIPS/arXiv work (`2512.20086`) extends OMTAD into a graph-based maritime-anomaly benchmark. The actual data artifact + its license are **UNVERIFIED** here — pull the data/repo links from the arXiv "Code & Data" section or contact the authors before relying on it. It is one of the only purpose-built, *labeled* maritime-anomaly benchmarks, so it is worth the chase for NV063 evaluation.

## TOP 3 PICKS for THESEUS

1. **MarineCadastre.gov AIS (US, public domain).** The single safest, largest, cleanest commercial-OK corpus. Public-domain status removes all SBIR licensing risk, the standardized fields drop straight into the fusion pipeline, and US coastal/port congestion exercises the NV063 "congested environment" requirement at scale. **Primary training + volume backbone.**

2. **Ushant TSS AIS (CC BY 4.0) + Piraeus AIS (CC BY 4.0, multi-modal).** A commercial-OK European pair: Ushant gives a small, clean, structured-lane set perfect for **cold-start POL** validation and synthetic lane/direction-violation anomalies; Piraeus adds 244M records of congested-port traffic **with bundled weather + synopses**, the best open substrate for **multi-source fusion** experiments. Together they cover the "learn normal in a novel area, then flag deviation" core build-beat with permissive licenses.

3. **OMTAD / NeurIPS'25 graph anomaly benchmark (license UNVERIFIED — chase it).** THESEUS needs a *labeled* anomaly benchmark to report honest NV063 numbers (precision/recall, base rates, baselines per HONESTY-FIRST). OMTAD and its 2025 graph extension are the closest open, purpose-built maritime-anomaly benchmarks found. Confirm the data artifact + license; if it lands as commercial-OK or research-eval-OK, it becomes the headline benchmark for the SBIR.

> **Honorable mention / research-only:** **Global Fishing Watch** has the richest *anomaly labels* (dark-vessel AIS gaps, loitering, encounters) but is **NON-COMMERCIAL (CC BY-NC-SA)** — usable for internal research/benchmarking only, never in a shipped product or for-profit SBIR deliverable without separate GFW permission. **Kystverket (NLOD)** is a strong commercial-OK fourth pick for open-ocean/high-latitude coverage.

## Access mechanics / gotchas (cross-cutting)

- **Commercial-OK shortlist for product + SBIR:** MarineCadastre (public domain), Ushant (CC BY 4.0), Piraeus (CC BY 4.0, confirm tag), Kystverket open feed (NLOD), DMA (free/open, confirm policy string). **Avoid in shippable deliverables:** Global Fishing Watch (NC), AISHub (reciprocal + no-redistribution), and any Kaggle upload whose provenance/license you have not individually verified.
- **Raw NMEA vs. decoded CSV:** DMA / MarineCadastre / Piraeus / Ushant ship **decoded CSV** (easy ingest). Kystverket / AISHub are **live streams** (raw IEC/NMEA) — useful for testing THESEUS's real-time ingest path, not for bulk training.
- **AIS coverage realities that matter for NV063:** terrestrial AIS (DMA, MarineCadastre, Kystverket-shore) thins out offshore; AIS gaps are themselves an anomaly signal (dark vessels) but also a sensor-coverage artifact — the radar fusion leg must disambiguate. Plan to *synthesize* spoofing / gap / loitering anomalies on the clean commercial-OK sets (Ushant, MarineCadastre) rather than depend on GFW's NC labels for shipped eval.
- **Cold-start alignment:** these sets are large *historical* corpora; THESEUS's no-historical-DB constraint means they should be used to **develop and benchmark** the online POL learner (replay them as streams), not embedded as a shipped reference DB.
- **Confirm-before-ship checklist:** capture the exact license string for DMA (data-management-policy page) and Piraeus (Zenodo license field), and resolve OMTAD's data artifact + license, before any of those three enters the SBIR compliance file.
